How do I manually test the operation of my garage door during a maintenance check?
To manually test your garage door, first disconnect the automatic opener by pulling the release cord. Then, lift the door manually to see if it opens and closes smoothly without resistance. This helps ensure the door is balanced and the springs are functioning properly.
What should I do if my garage door doesn’t move smoothly during manual operation?
If your garage door doesn’t move smoothly, check for any obstructions in the tracks, inspect the rollers for wear or damage, and ensure that the springs are intact. Lubricate moving parts with a silicone-based lubricant. If problems persist, consider calling a professional for further inspection.
Why is testing manual operation important during garage door maintenance?
Testing manual operation is crucial because it helps identify issues with the door’s balance, springs, and tracks that may not be apparent during automatic operation. Regular manual testing ensures safe and reliable door function, preventing potential damage and prolonging the door’s lifespan.
