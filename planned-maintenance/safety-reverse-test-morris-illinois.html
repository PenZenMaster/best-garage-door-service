What is the Safety Reverse Test for a garage door, and why is it important?
The Safety Reverse Test ensures that the garage door reverses direction if it encounters an obstruction while closing. It is important for preventing injuries and damage, ensuring the doors safety features are functioning properly.
How often should I perform a Safety Reverse Test on my garage door in Morris, Illinois?
It is recommended to perform a Safety Reverse Test at least once a month as part of regular garage door maintenance to ensure the safety mechanisms are working correctly.
What steps should I follow to perform a Safety Reverse Test on my garage door?
Place a 2x4 board flat on the ground in the doors path and close the door. If the door does not automatically reverse after hitting the board, the auto-reverse mechanism needs adjustment or repair.
What should I do if my garage door fails the Safety Reverse Test?
If the door fails the test, consult a professional garage door technician in Morris, Illinois, to inspect and repair the auto-reverse mechanism to ensure it complies with safety standards.
