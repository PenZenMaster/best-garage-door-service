What are the signs that my garage door springs need to be replaced?
Signs include visible gaps in the spring, the door not opening smoothly or staying in place, excessive noise during operation, and the door appearing crooked or unbalanced.
How often should garage door springs be replaced as part of planned maintenance?
Garage door springs typically last 7-9 years with regular use, but it’s advisable to inspect them annually and replace them every 5-7 years if signs of wear are evident to prevent sudden failure.
Can I replace garage door springs myself, or should I hire a professional?
It’s recommended to hire a professional, as replacing garage door springs can be dangerous due to the high tension they are under. A professional ensures safe and correct installation.
What type of springs should I use for replacement in New Lenox, Illinois?
The type of springs needed depends on your garage door system. Common types include torsion springs and extension springs. It’s best to consult with a local professional who can recommend the proper springs based on your door’s specifications and local climate conditions.
